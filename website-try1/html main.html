300 Miles to Heaven (1989)

One of the most controversial films of 80’s Polish cinema was hidden from the public from many years. The film predicts a butterfly effect of socialism and the Fall of the Berlin Wall on a Polish Millennial from Generation Y. The story is similar to the myth of Daedalus and Icarus and explores the idea of freedom and its consequences while simultaneously dealing with a subject of emigration and belonging.

Rams (2015)

In Grímur Hákonarson’s Rams, two lumbering, middle-aged brothers, Gummi (Sigurður Sigurjónsson) and Kiddi (Theodór Júlíusson), tend sheep in a remote valley. The animals are of the same ancestral stock their family has reared for aeons, and while the men haven’t spoken to each other for 40 years, thanks to an amusingly unspecified grudge, for the most part they rub along, passing handwritten notes via a helpful sheepdog when a grunt or a glower won’t suffice.

Oldboy (2003)

is a 2003 Korean vengeance thriller and the fourth feature film by Korean director Park Chan-Wook. At the time of its release, it was highly acclaimed, winning numerous awards in South Korea and Asia as well as the Grand Prix at the 2004 edition of the Cannes film festival. The film is the second instalment of Park Chan-Wook’s ‘Vengeance trilogy’.
